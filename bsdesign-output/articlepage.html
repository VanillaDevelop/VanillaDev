The Premise

As video games have evolved as a medium over the past decades, so has their monetization. Over the last years, the most prevalent change has been the introduction of “microtransactions” (short: MTX) – small purchases within the game itself unlocking additional content or increasing the power level of the player character.

In MMORPGs, a genre of video games which is mostly known for large, persistent worlds with thousands to millions of concurrent players, MTX have been a point of contention for many years. Unlike in singleplayer games, the power level of a few players can inadvertently affect the game balance for all other players, and can sometimes even completely shift the economy of the world. Players thus often advocate for a “pay2win free” environment, where “pay2win” (or p2w) refers to the ability to purchase tangible advantages within the game with real money.

Interestingly, this sentiment is somewhat regional to Europe and North America. In Asia, on the other hand, players are often much more accepting of such practices. This discrepancy was very clearly shown this year in the summer launch of “Aion Classic” – a western relaunch of an MMORPG by Korean Developer and Publisher NCSoft.

Article Text

As a basis for this discussion, the same dataset which is provided on Kaggle is used, which covers posts between the 9th of June and the 15th of July. However, you can re-run the scraping yourself using the R script provided on GitHub to obtain more recent data. To first understand the general conversation on the forums scraped, wordclouds and semantic networks can be used.

Wordclouds are likely something most data scientists will be familiar with. They simply show words scaled by their frequency in the corpus (in this case, all posts in the forums). We can use a wordcloud to get a general feel for the type of posts within the forums. The provided wordcloud is corrected to remove stopwords (common english words such as “the”, “a”, “I”, etc.) – some trends can already be seen in this picture. The main words discussing the game are of course present (“classic”, “aion”, “game”), but other points of discussion can also be identified, such as “candies”, “subscription”, “p2w”, “bots”…

One interesting note is the appearance of the words (“can”, “people”/”players”, “just”), which could potentially be a phrase commonly used together. This will be inspected in a further step.
