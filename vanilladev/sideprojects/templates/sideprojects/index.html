Other Work

Besides the occasional full-scope project and article, there are also several other deliverables I have created over the years. For one reason or another, these projects are either limited in scope, written in inconsistent code style, or more precise details of the implementation either are not available anymore, or cannot be made available. These projects are grouped into three distinct categories and are showcased briefly on this page.

Professional Work

These are projects that I've worked on within the scope of university or in cooperation with companies, for example in internships. Usually, even when I'm not legally obligated to keep the code and finer implementation details of such projects closed source, I will do so anyway in order to prevent any future potential complications.

Side Projects

Side projects are projects with a relatively small scope and/or relatively messy code, due to being built while learning a new tool or framework, or due to an inherently limited use case. If a coding project does not warrant a full coverage of its features, functionality, and technology stack, I will usually classify it as a side project, and list it here briefly.

The Treasure Trove

The Treasure Trove is a collection of projects that I have worked on in my teenage years. These projects are often not worth showing off on a technical level, but are things I was passionate about when I just started programming, and to me are worth preserving on a sentimental level. Unlike the others, this collection will likely not grow!
